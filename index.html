The CPDC Classification System

Understanding the four dimensions of modern orthodontic diagnosis

D1: Dental Alignment

Comprehensive assessment of tooth position, alignment, and arch relationships

Grading Scale

A - Ideal Perfect alignment, no discrepancies
B - Mild Minor crowding/spacing < 3mm
C - Moderate Noticeable discrepancies 3-6mm
D - Severe Significant issues 6-9mm
E - Extreme Major discrepancies > 9mm

AI Analysis Parameters

  • Crowding and spacing measurements
  • Tooth rotation and inclination
  • Arch form and symmetry
  • Bolus displacement analysis

D2: Skeletal Relationship

Evaluation of jaw relationships and skeletal patterns

Grading Scale

A - Ideal Balanced skeletal relationship
B - Mild Minor skeletal discrepancies
C - Moderate Clear skeletal imbalance
D - Severe Significant skeletal disharmony
E - Extreme Major skeletal deformity

AI Analysis Parameters

  • Cephalometric landmark detection
  • ANB angle and Wits appraisal
  • Vertical and transverse analysis
  • Airway and soft tissue assessment

D3: Facial Aesthetics

Assessment of facial harmony, profile, and smile aesthetics

Grading Scale

A - Ideal Excellent facial harmony
B - Mild Minor aesthetic concerns
C - Moderate Noticeable aesthetic issues
D - Severe Significant aesthetic compromise
E - Extreme Major facial disharmony

AI Analysis Parameters

  • Facial symmetry analysis
  • Golden proportion assessment
  • Smile line and dental display
  • Lip competence and profile

D4: Functional Occlusion

Evaluation of occlusal function, guidance, and TMJ health

Grading Scale

A - Ideal Optimal functional occlusion
B - Mild Minor functional issues
C - Moderate Clear functional disturbances
D - Severe Significant functional problems
E - Extreme Major functional pathology

AI Analysis Parameters

  • Occlusal interference detection
  • Guidance pattern analysis
  • Joint vibration analysis
  • Muscle activity assessment
